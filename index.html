1) Describe how your dashboard answers the questions presented. You don't have to address every question directly, but should at a high level address the main questions. (10 points)

Our boss wants to know the top 10 video games of all time or top 10 for a specific year. In the top left graph, we answer exactly that. The bar graph shows the 10 highest selling video games for each year (or all time when the "Show all years" button is toggled), with the names of the video games on the y axis and the sales count as the x axis.
Our boss also wants to know which genre is most popular. In the bottom left graph, we answer exactly that. The map shows the top selling video games genre by region (North America, Japan, Europe, and Other) for the corresponding time-range. Hovering over each country will display the genre most popular in that region.
Finally, our boss wants to know which publisher to use for each genre of game. In the right-hand graph, we answer exactly that. The lollipop graph displays the total number of sales by the leading publisher for each genre for the corresponding time frame. Calculating the publisher with the most sales by genre by year felt like a very reasonable means to determine this result.

2) List 3 reasons why D3 was helpful and improved your visualization (6 points)

- D3 made it incredibly easy to link data with svg objects (would be a pain to do with plain js)
- D3 has great helper functions that allowed me to store all relevant graph data in one location (rather than having to isolate my html from css from js, everything graph-related is rendered in one place)
- D3 allows me to dynamically update my data in real time, without having to refer to a server or needing to rerender the page in order to update the data

3) List 3 reasons why D3 would not be the best tool for creating a visualization (6 points)

- D3 could theoretically be a problem if you are concerned about the size of your file, without optimizations it can be rendering over 70+ KB of code, which can lead to worse SEO results
- If we don't want to expose our csv or data to the user, we shouldn't be using a client facing library to render graphs, and it may be better to do all of our graph-related work on the backend so users only can see what we show them
- D3 was created at a time when websites were rendered through the DOM and libaries like jquery reigned supreme. Over the last 10 years, a lot has changed in modern web applications, and most modern applications use a tool like REACT to interact with a virtual DOM to update state. Under these instances, using D3 could prove problematic when trying to use these two tools together.

4) Evaluate the accessibility of your dashboard based on the readings in the “Before you begin” section. What kinds of users might find this dashboard accessible and who might have more difficulty? What additional actions might you take to make this dashboard more accessible to all audiences? Your response should refer to at least one of the readings and be about 1 paragraph.

I spent a decent amount of time ensure that color blind users would be able to effectively read my graphs. In particular, I made sure to use coolors.co and other online resources to make graphs based on different shades of the same color to ensure that users can effectively distinguish between colors. Additionally, all of my graphs are very well labelled, with tooltips available on the two left-hand graphs as well to effectively ensure they can read any information that users could theoretically have issues with visually. I did not, however, take advantage of assistive technology (as mentioned in the Schepers reading), and this may make it more diffiuclt for blind internet-users to parse through my data. Fortunately, most of my graphs are very well labelled, but without "alt text" descriptions these users will most likely have a more difficult time interpreting my data.

5) Reflect on the stages of your design and implementation process when you could have taken steps to make your dashboard more accessible to all audiences. What are some factors that kept you from taking these steps? (a few sentences)

Again, providing alt text descriptions could have been a good step to make my dashboard more accessible for all audiences. A few factors kept me from taking these steps. Primarily, I wanted to ensure I could build a map graph and complete the assignment in time. With more time, I could go back to complete these steps, but given the time crunch and since this is my first time building something with D3 I didn't want to take unnecesary risks with regards to producing working code in the proper time frame.
